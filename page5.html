Ganapathy English Medium School

PreviousNext

        In the very heart of MaṅgaĪūru, housed in a fiery red-stone heritage building stands the bustling, historic Gaṇapathy High School (GHS). Its mere mention invokes awe amongst some and cherished memories amongst the other old students of Gaṇapathy High School. A legendary institution that began as the “Sāraswath School” from the humble precincts of the Umā Maheshwara Temple in 1870, it went on to become a model school with a curriculum prescribed by the Madras Presidency, of which Mangalore was then a part. In 1909, the school was officially named Gaṇapathy Secondary School and found itself operating from a ramshackle barrack-style building on Field Street.  


        The School shifted to its present 2-acre campus at Hampankaṭṭā, in 1915. A long overdue upgrade came in 1918 when it was designated a High School with carpentry and weaving being introduced as crafts, and its students even won certificates of merit in far-away places like the Trichūr Exhibition. By 1924 the winds of British-style modernisations were sweeping through the Indian education edifice and the Gaṇapathy High School was no exception. A children’s block, co-education, scouts, and new systems of teaching were rapidly introduced.
With an increasing demand for English-medium education in MaṅgaĪūru, Gaṇapathy English Medium School (GEMS) was started in 1989. For a few years the Gaṇapathy High School and Gaṇapathy English Medium School were run in parallel. Over a period of time, the huge demand and popularity of English-medium education over the local medium of instruction led to the slow decline of admissions for the Kannaḍa-medium Gaṇapathy High School.
Present - Gaṇapathy English Medium School

        The Sāraswat Education Society inaugurated the English-medium Gaṇapathy English Medium School on 1st June 1989. It currently offers student classes Nursery/KG to 10th Standard. Computers, Physics and Chemistry laboratories, a library, games facilities etc are provided for the overall development of the students of the school. Subsidized meals, security and safety for all students, hygienic and clean toilets, well maintained landscape gardens, guest lectures for the students, and yoga are some of the initiatives undertaken here.

        Students are encouraged to actively participate in outreach activities as a part of the focus on cultural, social and moral awareness. The Swachha MaṅgaĪūru Abhiyān in 2016 saw the enthusiastic participation of 300 students and faculty over 10 consecutive Sundays, where they hit the road to give them an unprecedented clean-up. The old bus-stand and the railway station too got a sound scrubbing at the hands of an energetic student clean-up squads. Their efforts were greatly appreciated by the public at large and received wide coverage in the local Media. The school also celebrates events like the Karnataka Rajyotasava Day and Independence Day with a lot of fervour.
In a step to encourage holistic development, students are offered the opportunity to learn music in association with the Shaṅkar Mahādevan Academy. The school endeavours to instil cultural values in the children through celebration of various festivals
On the Academic front, the quiet, sincere commitment of the School Management and faculty has yielded some spectacular results: distinctions and first classes in the SSLC Examination, laurels earned by students in sports and cultural activities, and even a stamp of approval from the Block Education Officer for the School’s excellence on all fronts.
